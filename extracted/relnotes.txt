Introduction to ITIL (Version 5)
• ITIL has a strong global brand, adopted and adapted by organizations and professionals
  worldwide, thanks to the continuous involvement, active engagement, and co-creation with our
  community.

• While we recognize that parts of the ecosystem may remain aligned to earlier ITIL versions, this
  continued adoption is a testament to the stickiness of the ITIL brand.

• ITIL (Version 5) provides a new opportunity to further expand ITIL’s global reach and demonstrate
  how it can truly empower organizations and people to lead transformation with confidence.

• ITIL has consistently been the Global Best Practice Framework across its successive focus
  areas, evolving from IT Service Management (ITSM) to Service Management (SM) and Digital
  Transformation. While ITSM and SM (including Enterprise SM) remain our long-established
  leading areas, we are expanding the ITIL scope and positioning as the Global Best
  Practice framework for Digital Product and Service Management in the AI-driven world.

• Digital Product and Service Management: In today’s AI-driven world, all products and services
  are influenced by digital technologies, either directly or indirectly. The term ‘digital products
  and services’ captures the full spectrum of digital relevance; from traditionally non-digital
  products enabled or supported by digital technology during their lifecycle (e.g. a pen designed
  or produced using digital technologies), to technology-enabled products (e.g. a smartphone
  enabled by its operating system and installed applications), and further to products that
  are fully digital by nature (e.g. software). Digital services are delivered through, and are
  fundamentally dependent on, these digital products. As such, digital products and services
  must be approached holistically. The new ITIL unifies how organisations design, manage,
  and continuously improve digital products and services – enabling superior experiences,
  coherent strategies, and growth.

• Fundamental ITIL concepts and principles remain the same, slightly adjusted or simply
  repositioned in this new context.

• A relevant example is the positioning of the ITIL Management Practices. All 34 ITIL
  Management Practices remain unchanged and continue to sit at the core of the ITIL framework as
  essential value enablers. However, in the new ITIL Foundation, the focus has shifted away from
  memorizing knowledge related to individual practices towards understanding their
  purpose, structure, benefits, and their role within the ITIL Value System – empowering
  organizations and professionals to use them confidently from day one to drive growth.
